About Smart School

Why we built this programme

Engineering doesn't have to wait until university. It starts here, in a classroom, with a small blue board and a big question: what can you make?

Smart School exists to give Nigerian students their first experience of engineering — not reading about it, but actually doing it.

Too many students graduate without ever building anything. They know the theory, but they've never felt the moment when code they wrote makes a physical object move. That moment changes how you see yourself. Smart School is built around that moment.

🎯

What Smart School Is

A 5-session government robotics education initiative for primary and junior secondary school students. Each session is 2 hours. By the end, students program a real robot.

👥

Who It's For

Primary 3 to JSS 2 students — ages 8 to 14. No prior knowledge of electronics or programming required. Zero experience is the starting point, not a barrier.

📍

Where It Happens

In-person sessions at participating schools and learning centres across Nigeria, facilitated by trained student engineers from universities like FUTA.

🏆

What Students Leave With

A working robot they programmed themselves, a foundational understanding of C++, and — most importantly — the belief that they can build things.

The 5-Session Journey

✓

Session 1 — Introduction to Arduino

The Input → Processing → Output model. Board anatomy. First C++ program. Making an LED blink.

✓

Session 2 — Outputs & Actuators

External LED, buzzer, servo motor, RGB LED. Digital vs analog. The Servo library. Colour mixing.

3

Session 3 — Sensors & Inputs

Ultrasonic sensor, IR sensor, buttons. Reading the world. Combining sensors with outputs.

4

Session 4 — Combining Inputs & Outputs

If-else logic. Reactive systems. Motor control. Making the robot respond to the environment.

🤖

Session 5 — Project Day

Students design, build, and demo their own project using the Elegoo Smart Car. Fully student-led.

Axora Labs — the organisation behind Smart School

Axora Labs is a student-led engineering and innovation organisation co-founded at FUTA. Its mission is to empower African engineers to build — through workshops, skill development programmes, and community. Smart School is the outreach arm of that mission, taking engineering education to younger students who've never had access to it.
